Tech Pro Tips: Essential Advice for Keeping Your Computer Safe

General Computer Security Tips

  • Install a Reliable Antivirus: Always keep it updated for the latest virus definitions.
  • Enable Automatic Updates: Ensure your operating system and applications are patched against vulnerabilities.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Combine upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid common words or personal information.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security for sensitive accounts.
  • Don't Click on Suspicious Links: Always verify the source before clicking links in emails or messages.

Protecting Your Data

  • Regular Backups: Use external hard drives or cloud services to back up important files weekly.
  • Encrypt Sensitive Files: Use tools like BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (Mac) to secure private data.
  • Avoid USB Drives from Unknown Sources: They might contain malicious software.
  • Organize Your Files: This makes it easier to identify if something has gone missing or been tampered with.

Internet Safety

  • Use a Secure Browser: Choose browsers with built-in privacy features like Firefox or Brave.
  • Install Ad Blockers: Prevent harmful ads that can deliver malware.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Activities: Use a VPN if you must connect.
  • Verify Website Authenticity: Look for HTTPS and check for typos in URLs.
  • Use Private Browsing Modes: Reduce your online footprint.

Email and Social Media Precautions

  • Don't Open Attachments from Unknown Senders: These could contain viruses or ransomware.
  • Review Privacy Settings: Limit what others can see on your social media profiles.
  • Be Wary of "Too Good to Be True" Offers: Scams are rampant on email and social platforms.
  • Avoid Sharing Sensitive Information Publicly: Keep personal and financial information private.
